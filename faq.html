Frequently Asked Questions About Kijiji Ontario

Understanding how to effectively use Kijiji Ontario can mean the difference between a successful transaction and a frustrating experience. These frequently asked questions address the most common concerns from both buyers and sellers across Ontario's diverse communities.

From posting your first ad in London to negotiating prices in Windsor, these answers draw on real user experiences and best practices developed over years of classified transactions. The platform continues to evolve, but core principles of clear communication, reasonable pricing, and personal safety remain constant.

What is Kijiji Ontario?

Kijiji Ontario is a popular online classifieds platform where users in Ontario, Canada can buy, sell, and trade goods and services locally. The platform operates as a digital marketplace connecting neighbors and community members across cities like Toronto, Ottawa, London, Hamilton, Windsor, and Brampton. Unlike national retailers or shipping-based marketplaces, Kijiji emphasizes local transactions where buyers can inspect items in person before purchasing. The service hosts millions of active listings across categories including vehicles, real estate, jobs, pets, furniture, electronics, and services. Most basic listings are free to post, making it accessible to individuals, small businesses, and anyone looking to declutter or find deals within their community.

How do I post an ad on Kijiji Ontario?

Create a free Kijiji account by providing an email address and creating a password. Once logged in, click the 'Post Ad' button typically located in the upper right corner of the website. Select the appropriate category for your item - choosing the right category increases visibility to interested buyers. Fill in all required fields including a descriptive title, detailed description, asking price, and your location. Upload 3-8 clear, well-lit photos showing the item from multiple angles, as listings with quality images receive 5 times more responses than those without. Include specific details like dimensions, brand names, condition, and any defects. Review your listing for accuracy, then click publish. Your ad typically appears within 15 minutes and remains active for 60 days unless renewed or deleted.

Is Kijiji Ontario free to use?

Yes, posting most ads on Kijiji Ontario is free, though some premium features and promoted listings may require payment. Standard classified ads in categories like furniture, electronics, household items, and most services cost nothing to post. However, certain categories including real estate listings and job postings may require fees ranging from $5 to $40 depending on the listing type and duration. Kijiji also offers optional paid features like 'Top Ad' placement that keeps your listing at the top of search results, or 'Urgent' tags that highlight your ad with special formatting. These promotional features typically cost $7-15 for multi-day visibility boosts. Browsing, searching, and contacting sellers remains completely free for all users.

What can I buy and sell on Kijiji Ontario?

You can buy and sell almost anything including cars, electronics, furniture, real estate, jobs, services, and household items. Popular categories include classic cars, with Ontario sellers listing vintage vehicles from the 1960s through 1990s. Motorcycle parts and complete bikes attract enthusiasts looking for both modern and vintage components. Sailboats and watercraft sell particularly well given Ontario's extensive coastline and lake access. The platform also handles apartment rentals, room shares, and real estate listings. Pet adoptions and rehoming services connect animals with new families, though users should verify breeder credentials. Services from snow removal to web design allow freelancers to find clients. Prohibited items include weapons, illegal substances, counterfeit goods, and items that violate Canadian law.

How do I stay safe when using Kijiji Ontario?

Meet buyers and sellers in public places during daylight hours, preferably in high-traffic areas like coffee shops, shopping mall parking lots, or designated safe exchange zones that some police stations provide. Bring a friend or family member to transactions, especially for high-value items. Inspect items thoroughly before purchasing and test electronics to verify functionality. Never send money through wire transfers, cryptocurrency, or prepaid cards to people you haven't met. Avoid sharing personal information like your home address, banking details, or social insurance number. Trust your instincts - if a deal seems too good to be true or a buyer/seller acts suspiciously, walk away. For vehicle purchases, request a Used Vehicle Information Package and consider having a mechanic inspect the car before buying. Use cash for in-person transactions or secure payment methods like PayPal for shipped items.

How quickly do items typically sell on Kijiji Ontario?

Sale speed varies dramatically based on price, condition, location, and demand. Underpriced items in popular categories like electronics or furniture often sell within 24-48 hours, sometimes receiving multiple inquiries within the first hour of posting. Vehicles priced at or below market value typically generate responses within 2-3 days, while classic cars may take 2-4 weeks to find the right collector. Seasonal items sell fastest during peak demand periods - snowblowers in November, air conditioners in June, and boats in April-May. Location significantly impacts speed: items in Toronto or Ottawa with populations over 1 million sell faster than those in smaller cities. Niche items like specialized tools or collectibles may take 30-60 days to reach the right buyer. Improving your listing with better photos, detailed descriptions, and competitive pricing can reduce selling time by 40-60%.

What's the best way to price items on Kijiji Ontario?

Research completed sales and current listings for identical or similar items in your area to establish market value. Price used items at 40-60% of original retail for good condition, 25-40% for fair condition, and 10-25% for items with significant wear or defects. Account for age - electronics depreciate rapidly with 1-year-old items worth 50-60% of retail, while quality furniture holds value better. Consider your urgency: price 10-15% below market for quick sales, at market value for standard timing, or slightly above if you're willing to wait for the right buyer. Build in negotiation room by pricing 10-20% higher than your minimum acceptable price, as most buyers expect to negotiate. For vehicles, consult Canadian Black Book or similar resources for accurate valuations. Seasonal timing matters - price winter items higher in fall, summer items higher in spring. Free items generate immediate interest but attract unreliable respondents, so consider asking $5-10 for low-value items to ensure serious inquiries.

Can I use Kijiji Ontario to find legitimate job opportunities?

Yes, but exercise caution and verify employer legitimacy before providing personal information or attending interviews. Legitimate jobs on Kijiji include positions with small local businesses, construction and trades work, restaurant and retail positions, and freelance opportunities that might not appear on corporate job boards. Red flags include jobs requiring upfront fees, positions promising unrealistic income, requests for banking information before hiring, interviews conducted entirely through text or email, and vague job descriptions. Verify company existence through online searches, check for professional email addresses rather than free services like Gmail, and research the business address. Never pay for job placements, training materials, or background checks. Legitimate employers cover these costs. The platform works well for finding local, entry-level, and gig economy work, but professional positions are better sourced through dedicated employment websites. Always meet in public places for initial interviews and trust your instincts about suspicious opportunities.
